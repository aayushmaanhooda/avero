## 6. Company Themes and Public Thought Leadership

### 6.1 AI-Native Business
Avreo’s public messaging repeatedly uses the idea of becoming **AI-native**.

This means using AI as a core part of business design, not just as a small tool added to existing workflows.

A public post uses the Jetstar example to explain business reinvention. The idea is that companies may need to create new AI-native models instead of forcing old organisations and systems to behave like modern AI-powered businesses.

Key ideas:

- AI changes the speed and cost of business reinvention
- A small AI-powered team can do work that previously required many layers of coordination
- Businesses should ask where disruption is coming from
- Companies should consider whether their existing moat is still defensible
- Organisations may need to disrupt themselves before the market does

### 6.2 AI Roadmaps
Avreo’s public posts argue that AI roadmaps should include:

- Clear business outcomes
- Prioritised use cases with ROI
- People and process design
- Data readiness
- Buy/build/broker decisions
- Scale, security, and interoperability
- Clear delivery ownership
- Success measurement
- Support model
- Fit-for-purpose technology foundations

The key message is that too many AI roadmaps fail because they over-index on technology and under-index on outcomes, process, people, and value.

### 6.3 AI ROI
Avreo’s public posts emphasise ROI-first AI adoption.

Important ROI considerations:

- Current cost of delivering a service
- Expected AI-driven efficiency uplift
- New cost of delivery after AI
- Resulting ROI
- Time savings
- Margin uplift
- Profit improvement

The public message is that the most valuable AI use cases are often practical, operational, and behind the scenes rather than flashy demos.

### 6.4 AI Policy and Governance
Avreo’s public posts argue that good AI policies should:

- Protect the organisation and its data
- Extend information security policies
- Encourage positive AI usage
- Support productivity and innovation
- Be practical and measurable
- Be enforceable
- Involve business-unit consultation
- Evolve as AI technology, regulations, and business models change

The public view is that AI policy should not be written only from fear. It should enable safe and productive AI usage.

### 6.5 AI Architecture: Single LLM vs Multi-Model Orchestration
Avreo’s public posts discuss the difference between:

- Using one LLM end-to-end
- Orchestrating multiple models with different roles

The key idea is that different models may be better suited for different functions, such as drafting, reviewing, risk detection, compliance checking, code testing, or domain-specific reasoning.

Avreo’s view is that model orchestration can be important for trustworthy, scalable, enterprise-ready AI systems.

### 6.6 RAG vs Long-Context Prompting
Avreo’s public posts discuss how long-context LLMs change the role of RAG.

Key idea:

- RAG was originally useful because models could not fit large knowledge bases into context.
- With larger context windows, some use cases may use context-only prompting.
- However, precision still matters.
- Organisations may need both RAG and context-only strategies depending on the use case.

Suggested interpretation:

- RAG remains useful for precision, freshness, retrieval control, access control, and structured knowledge systems.
- Long-context prompting may be useful when the full corpus can be safely and cheaply included.

### 6.7 AI-Ready Data
Avreo’s public posts argue that businesses do not always need huge data transformation programs before using AI.

The message is that organisations can sometimes connect AI directly to business systems and ask natural-language questions such as:

- What were closed sales for July?
- How often do customers buy a specific product?
- What sales uplift should we expect from a promotion?

Avreo publicly mentions partnering with an AI-ready data platform called **Apiro** that connects to systems such as Salesforce and Xero and turns them into natural-language-ready data sources.
